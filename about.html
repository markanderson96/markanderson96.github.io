About Me

Hello!

I am Mark Anderson and I am a PhD student in the SIGMEDIA Group at Trinity College Dublin.
I am studying under the supervision of Dr. Naomi Harte and my research is on the automatic and remote monitoring of birds through their vocalisations.
My background is primarily in signal processing (originally analogue but now mostly digital) and now moving slightly more into the deep learning sphere,
although I like to stick to signal processing whenever I can (deep learning is a useful tool, but not the be all end all!).
I got my Bachelors from Trinity in 2018, followed by my masters degree in 2019.

Besides my research, I am interested in music (I play concertina, guitar and do a bit of music production/synth work), retro/vintage computing,
electronics (also as a hobby), ham radio, photography and video games. So you may see some projects relating to those here, or on github in general.
Software wise I like to mess around with various deep learning methods for synthesis, or code up useful tools for myself, or sometimes tools that aren't useful but were fun anyway!
I do hope to add more hardware projects soon, I have some test equipment designs, synthesisers/eurorack modules, blinky things. Fun things like that.
